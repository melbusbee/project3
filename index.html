How safe are our nation's children from abuse?


Child victims of abuse, neglect and maltreatment can be found in every state across the nation, with 678,810 victims reported in 2012 alone. An estimated 1,640 children died from abuse and neglect at the hands of parents, relatives, caregivers and neighbors, and that number has been increasing since 2010. Both women and men abuse and neglect children, and many do it more than once. Eighty-one percent of victims were maltreated by a parent(s), and 80 percent of child fatalities were caused by one or both parents. Young children are the most vulnerable to maltreatment. In 2012, more than 26 percent (or 181,493) of victims reported were younger than 3 years of age. Except for sexual abuse, victims under 2 years of age had the largest percentages across all maltreatment types.
Some of the most severe outcomes are seen in the South. Southern states (such as Texas, Florida, Georgia, Louisiana, and Arkansas) reported a higher proportion of victims resulting in fatality than non-Southern states. In general, the rate of victimization across all states decreased with age, with a significant drop (of nearly 60%) from infant victims (under age 1) to victims age 1. But, Arizona, Louisiana and Tennessee report a higher proportion of infants victimized than all other states.

THE DEFINITION


What is abuse/neglect?

Any recent act or failure to act on the part of a parent or caretaker which results in death, serious physical or emotional harm, sexual abuse or exploitation; or an act or failure to act, which presents an imminent risk of serious harm.
